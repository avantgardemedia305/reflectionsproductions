01 · Three Ways to Run It

Basic. Standard.
BEAST.

The GEN3 mast-column design is the strongest hydraulic stage the APEX line has built — and it deploys in three operational formats depending on how much show you're flying.

Format 01

Basic 5040

  • Two 8′ fly bays
  • Travels as one self-contained trailer at legal weight
  • Crew of 6 · set up and strike in 3 hours
Format 02

Standard 5040

  • Two 12′ fly bays
  • Same single-trailer transport
  • Crew of 6 · set up and strike in 3 hours
Format 03

“BEAST” 5040

  • Two 25′ fly bays · 10,000 lbs each · no ground support
  • Flies a 90′×6′ video banner, two 18′×24′ IMAG walls + 10,000 lb upstage wall
  • Extended sound bays at 4,000 lbs each
  • Full up/downstage spanner-beam system — fly points almost anywhere under the roof
  • Additional transport · crew of 11

04 · High Wind Action Plan

Engineered for
Florida Weather

The GEN3 ships with a formal engineer-reviewed High Wind Action Plan (HWAP) under the 2018 International Building Code — the document a Florida fire marshal or building official asks about first.

  • Defines separate rated wind design speeds for an open roof system (no walls or flown equipment) and a fully enclosed roof system (side/back walls or roof-hung equipment installed).
  • Establishes wind-monitoring and escalation procedures during venue operation, so the stage crew has a defined, safe response as wind loads increase.
  • Prescribes wall-panel removal as the method for reducing projected wind area on the main roof — global stability is preserved through the event.
  • Structural analysis by Entertainment Structures Group; site-specific permit review by a structural engineer as required by the venue's jurisdiction.

The complete HWAP, structural calculations, drawing approvals and flame-resistance certificates are in the downloadable package — and ride with the stage to every show.

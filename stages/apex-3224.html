Overview

The APEX 3224 is the go-to stage for mid-size festivals, city concerts and corporate productions. The 34-foot trailer rides on a gooseneck tongue that connects to standard ball or kingpin hitches, and the hydraulic lift puts the stage up in under an hour.

A reversible front is the 3224's trick: both sides of the stage can serve as the face, which gives site planners real flexibility for orientation, sightlines and water drainage. The reinforced roof and rigging capacity carry lighting, audio and LED displays for a fully produced look.

It's also the fleet's broadcast specialist: the 3224 is the same stage the major sports networks use for their “live on the scene” sets — ESPN College GameDay-style builds covering auto racing, horse racing and college football. It can be placed and broadcast-ready within 30 minutes, with the roof set at variable heights for cameras and lighting grids.

The thesis

1You don't have a demand problem — you have a visibility problem.

South Florida produces hundreds of staged outdoor events a year. Reflections has 50 years of proof and 7 stages of inventory, and almost none of the buyers doing the hiring can find them or have heard from them.

2Stage inventory is perishable.

7 stages × ~30 high-season weekends ≈ 200+ stage-weekends a year. A stage that sits in the yard on an event weekend is revenue that expires. The Mission's job: fill dark weekends.

3The buying cycle is annual — so the plan is annual.

Municipal fiscal years turn over Oct 1. Festivals book 6–12 months out. Fair contracts lock right after the prior fair. A 3-month push can't touch a 12-month buying cycle; seeds planted in Q1 close in Q3 and Q4.

03 · The Lead Engine — Channels

Three lanes, one funnel. Everything reports to the same scoreboard.

No spray-and-pray. This market is a few hundred named buyers, not the general public — so the engine is built for precision: inbound capture for the people already searching, outbound pursuit for the buyers who should know Reflections, and relationships & bids for the contracts that never hit the open market.

Lane 1

Inbound — be found · runs from Phase 1

Website quote funnel: every page funnels to Get a Quote; call tracking number + form analytics so every lead has a source.
Google Business Profile: rebuilt listing (photos, services, stages as products, weekly posts). 42% of local searchers click the map-pack results (Backlinko 2024) — that's where "stage rental near me" money lands.
Review engine: systematic requests to 50 years of past clients, then every completed 2026-27 event. 97% of consumers read reviews before choosing a local business (BrightLocal 2026). Target: 25+ Google reviews by Oct, 40+ by renewal.
SEO (Phase 4 wave): 7 stage spec pages (product SEO), market pages (Miami / Ft. Lauderdale / West Palm / Orlando), resources hub ("what size stage do I need"), Backstage blog kept as News.
Google Ads (Phase 3): Search-only on high-intent keywords ("mobile stage rental," "stage rental Miami / Florida," "event production company Miami," "concert stage rental") + remarketing. Benchmarks: business-services category averages $5.58 CPC / ~5.1% conversion / ~$104 per lead (WordStream 2025); our niche terms est. $4–12 CPC. At $1,500–2,500/mo media we model 8–16 inbound leads/mo once tuned. Media billed at cost — separate pass-through budget, AGM manages it inside the retainer. (Verified: Google Local Services Ads has NO event/staging category — that shortcut doesn't exist; budget goes to standard Search.)
Marketplace listings (cheap tests, Phase 2): The Bash ($79–199/yr + 5% commission) and GigSalad (2.5–5% commission) — event-vendor marketplaces worth listing the fleet on. Thumbtack/Peerspace skipped: wrong category fit.
Social engine: 90 days already built (245 graphics, Jul 15–Oct 12); months 4–12 produced monthly on the same system — proof-of-work content: load-ins, timelapses, then-and-now.
Lane 2

Outbound — go get them · runs from Phase 2

Past-client reactivation FIRST: excavate 50 years of client records into the CRM (target 300+ contacts). "The New Reflections" announcement: email + personal calls + printed piece to the best 100. Warmest list in Florida — they already trusted the company once.
Named-account sequences: 150–200 target accounts across Tier 1–2, built from the hit list + procurement contacts. Sequenced, personalized email — 5–6 touches over 2–4 weeks (follow-ups drive 42% of all replies; most senders quit after one). Well-targeted small-list B2B earns 1–5%+ reply rates vs under 1% for generic sends (Belkins/Martal 2025-26). Why we don't "blast": Gmail/Yahoo sender rules now punish bulk volume, and this market is a few hundred named buyers anyway — precision beats volume on both deliverability and math.
Call blocks: 25–40 calls/week during Phase 2–3, scripts per vertical, calls follow email engagement (opens/clicks first). Industry blended benchmarks: ~10% connect per dial, ~25% per prospect with persistence, 58% of connects become real conversations (Belkins 2025-26). A named, warm-market list with a 50-year reputation is built to beat blended averages decisively.
Dimensional direct mail: printed fleet lookbook (spec sheets + hero shots) to the top 100 buyers (parks & rec directors, fair boards, festival EDs). Paper survives on a desk; a PDF doesn't. Dimensional mail pulls 6–12% response at $5–25/piece vs a 4.4% direct-mail average and ~0.1% for mass email (ANA/DMA 2025) — 100 lookbooks ≈ $1.5–2.5K printed, 6–12 warm responses expected from exactly the buyers we want. Tracked via QR + dedicated landing URL.

Channel reality check — set your own expectations

  • Blended cold-call worst case is ~370 dials per meeting (Belkins) — the named warm list should crush that, but budget call time accordingly.
  • Cold-email blended averages are under 1% reply (blasts) vs 1–5%+ for tight lists — if the client asks for "an email blast," the answer is no, and the deliverability + list-size logic on Tab 3 is the script.
  • Day-rate table is market estimates from rental pricing guides (industry is RFQ-only) — swap in Reflections' real rates in month one before anyone quotes the ROI math back to you.
  • Benchmarks are cited client-facing by source name only (WordStream, Belkins, ANA/DMA, BrightLocal); full source URLs live in the project doc — don't screen-share that.

If numbers disappoint at 90 days — pre-agreed pivots, don't improvise

  • Shift budget from mail → calls (or reverse) based on which converts.
  • Pull ads forward if inbound underperforms.
  • Add a second reactivation wave (different angle: "book your 2027 date").
